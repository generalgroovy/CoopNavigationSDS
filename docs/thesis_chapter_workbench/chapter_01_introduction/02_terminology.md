# Chapter 1: Introduction - Terminology

Purpose: chapter-local definitions and explanations. Define terms before using them in thesis prose.

## Spoken dialogue system

- Definition: A dialogue system that communicates through spoken input and/or spoken output.
- Explanation:
  - This thesis models it as a sequence of observable phases.

## Task-oriented dialogue

- Definition: Dialogue directed toward a concrete, externally checkable goal.
- Explanation:
  - The goal here is finding and accepting a valid route under constraints.

## Route-grounded evaluation

- Definition: Evaluation where route candidates are checked against a transport network.
- Explanation:
  - This supplies independent correctness evidence.

## Semi-success

- Definition: A completed dialogue with partial task achievement, such as a valid route but violated constraint.
- Explanation:
  - Semi-success is crucial because binary success hides informative failures.

## Term-Use Rules

- Use one term for one construct; avoid alternating synonyms for key variables.
- Do not use `success`, `route validity`, and `constraint satisfaction` interchangeably.
- Reserve `causal` for controlled comparisons or ablations; otherwise use `associated with` or `indicates`.
- Define abbreviations at first use and prefer full terms in headings.
