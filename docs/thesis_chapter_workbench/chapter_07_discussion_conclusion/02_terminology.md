# Chapter 7: Discussion and Conclusion - Terminology

Purpose: chapter-local definitions and explanations. Define terms before using them in thesis prose.

## Defensible inference

- Definition: A conclusion supported by the available evidence and stated with its denominator and limits.
- Explanation:
  - Use this phrase mentally when drafting results and discussion.

## Causal claim

- Definition: A claim that one factor caused another.
- Explanation:
  - Avoid unless the experiment isolates the factor through matched design or ablation.

## Descriptive association

- Definition: A relationship observed in the data without full causal control.
- Explanation:
  - Most broad configuration effects currently belong here.

## Future work

- Definition: A bounded extension beyond the thesis scope.
- Explanation:
  - Keep it realistic: human validation, real microphones, broader ASR/TTS, real networks.

## Term-Use Rules

- Use one term for one construct; avoid alternating synonyms for key variables.
- Do not use `success`, `route validity`, and `constraint satisfaction` interchangeably.
- Reserve `causal` for controlled comparisons or ablations; otherwise use `associated with` or `indicates`.
- Define abbreviations at first use and prefer full terms in headings.
